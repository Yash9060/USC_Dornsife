Welcome To Experiental Learning

  • The Joint Educational Project (JEP)

    Established in 1972, USC JEP is one of the oldest & largest service-learning progams in the US. Each year, more than 2,000 USC students enroll in one of JEP's service-learning, work-study, or volunteer programs. JEP offers opportunities in nearby schools, community-based organizations, health-care facilities & legal clinics. With over 60,000 past participants, JEP continues to draw students in by offering a combination of hands-on experience and reflective practice, enabling students to learn first-hand about the critical issues facing communities surrounding USC.

  • Office of Oversea Studies

    The Office of Overseas Studeis provides opportunities for students to study abroad for a semester or a year., Eligible students can choose among 55 academic programs in 29 countries. The 6-week summmer Pembroke-King's Prograamme at Cambridge University is also offered through this office. Financial aid and scholarships may be applied to the cost of semester and year programs.

  • Department of Physical Education

    This program at USC has a variety of opportunities for to improve general health and strength through fitness related and recreational activities classes. Courses are designed to introduce students to various aspects of health & physical education principles. Students experience a hands on learning environment by connecting theory & fundamental skill instruction with application of those skills. With this exposure, knowledge, and skill, students are engaged intellectually and physically and are better equipped to make choices regarding a healthy lifestyle.

  • The Levan Institute for Humanities and Ethics

    The Levan Institue for Humanities and Ethics is a center of student and faculty engagement in critical inquiry into the timeless values at the core of our humanity. The institute facilities multidisciplinary programs, events, forums, and student organiztions to promote moral reflection on the crucial issues of our time. Levan students are encouraged to make a positive impact across society and around the world.

  • USC Dornsife Off-Campus Short-Term Program

    USC Dornsife offers short-term academic programs in several different formats in both the United States and abroad. Students can take advantage of any one of our unique programmitic opportunities; "Spring Break", "Maymester", "Problems Without Passports" and "Department Summer Away" courses extend undergraduate learning outside the walls of the university to just about anywhere in the world. Students gain research experience, participate in problem-based learning courses, study in a forein country, and are able to add unique boost to future resumes.

  • Washington D.C. Semester Program

    Provides a unique, semester-long opportunity for USC students to study & work in the capital.Courses are drawn from areas with direct policy relevance such as foreign policy, politics, and the political process & economics. All students complete an internship with one of Washington's many policy-focused organizations, including government agencies, nongovernmental organizations, consulting firms and congressionakl offices. The D.C. program is open to students from all majors while mantaining a focus on practical policy.

  • Center for Active Learning and International Studies (CALIS)

    CALIS is an outreach project of the School of International Relations dedicated to advancing teaching excellence in international studies. Through our service-learning programs, USC students team-teach in local high schools and act as a driving force for academic innovation and socially relevant scholarship. As USC teams teach locally, CALIS is better able to reach globally to provide effective strategies and materials in international studies.

Our Stories

Here About What Others Are #Doing


Surviving the Zombie Apocalypse

As an antidote to our technology-obsessed world, USC Dornsife offers a Maymester course that allows students to appreciate the age-old skills that form the basis of civilization.


Bridge Building in Cuba

Economics major Nicholas Mockabee, alongside fellow Trojans, explored Old Havana, rode in vintage American cars along the Malecón, Havana’s fabled seafront promenade, and experienced the beauty of the country’s architecture. Meeting journalists, artists, doctors, religious leaders, faculty, writers and musicians, students were able to explore issues related to international relations between Cuba and the U.S. and to learn more about Cuba’s universal health-care system and high literacy levels.


Taking the Plunge

Lauren Otaguro, a senior majoring in neuroscience and minoring in environmental studies took her studies further while traveling to Guam and Palau. While diving in the Pacific Islands, Lauren conducted research on the marine biology located in remote islands. Learn more about how she took her environmental studies education out of the classroom and into the water.
